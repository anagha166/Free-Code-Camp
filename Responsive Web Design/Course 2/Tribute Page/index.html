Alexander Flemming

Creator of the world's first antibiotic

Information

  • 1881- Born in Lochfield farm in Ayrshire, Scotland

  • 1900- Served in the Military as a Private in the London Scottish Regiment

  • 1903- Enrolls in St. Mary's Hospital Medical School in Paddington

  • 1906- Graduates with distinction and joins the St. Mary's research department as an assistant bacteriologist

  • 1908- Earns a Bachelor of Science degree in Bacteriology

  • 1915- marries Nurse Sarah Marion McElroy

  • 1916- Serves in WW1 as Captain of the Army Medical Corps

  • 1918- Returns to St. Mary's with the goal of researching antibacterial agents

  • 1921- Discovers Lysozyme, an antimicrobial enzyme

  • 1928- Accidentally discovers Pencillin

  • 1940- Howard Florey and Ernst Borio Chain continue Flemming's research and develop a way of mass producing pencillin

  • 1941- Mass production of pencillin commences to treat infections on the front-lines of WW2

  • 1943- Elected member of the Royal Society

  • 1945- Awarded Nobel Prize in Medicine with Florey and Chain

  • 1949- Flemming's wife dies

  • 1953- Remarried to Dr. Amalia Koutsouri-Vourekas

  • 1955- Flemming dies and is buried in St. Paul's Cathedral
